Morphis

A creative innovation event that challenges students to think beyond imagination and transform ideas into impactful designs and solutions. A platform for students to showcase their originality, teamwork, and practical thinking while addressing modern-day concepts and themes. Participants will explore design thinking, environmental awareness, and creative expression through exciting competitive rounds.

Morphis

Competition
Overview

01

Round 1 – Design Creation

In the first round, participants will submit and present their concept, inspiration, and initial design idea based on the given theme through an online presentation. Teams are expected to showcase their creativity, innovation, and understanding of the theme through sketches, digital presentations, mood boards, or concept visuals. This round focuses on evaluating the originality of the idea, clarity of concept, creative thinking, and overall presentation. Shortlisted teams will advance to the next stage of the competition based on their performance and uniqueness of their proposed design solution.

The submission deadline for Round 1: 18th July, 2026

02

Round 2 – Idea Presentation

In this round, participants will present their product concept and explain the inspiration, purpose, and functionality behind their idea to the judges. Students are encouraged to showcase their creativity, innovation, and problem-solving approach through clear and confident presentations. Participants may also create and display prototypes, sketches, models, or sample designs to help demonstrate their concept more effectively. Judges will evaluate the uniqueness of the idea, presentation skills, practicality, sustainability, and overall execution of the concept.

03

Round 3 – Rapid Innovation Round

In the final round, shortlisted participants will face an exciting on-the-spot creative challenge designed to test their innovation, problem-solving, and design thinking skills. A surprise theme will be revealed during the event, and participants must brainstorm, conceptualize, and develop a product idea that effectively responds to the given topic within the allotted time. Teams will be evaluated based on originality, practicality, creativity, presentation, and how well their product connects to the theme. This round encourages participants to think quickly, adapt creatively, and transform spontaneous ideas into impactful solutions.


All entries must demonstrate a clear connection to SDG 7 – Affordable and Clean Energy. Participants should creatively incorporate themes of renewable energy, energy conservation, sustainability, innovation, or responsible energy use through their designs, performances, products, artwork, presentations, or concepts. The relevance to SDG 7 will be considered as part of the judging criteria.

Guidelines &
Constraints

01

Eligibility

Participation is open only to the specified grade categories.

02

Discipline

Participants must maintain discipline and follow coordinators’ instructions throughout the event.

03

Strict Originality

All work must be original. Plagiarism, copied content, or any AI-generated designs, concepts, mood boards, sketches, or presentations are strictly prohibited and will result in disqualification.

04

IP Rights & Exhibition

Designers retain full IP rights. Creatricks reserves the right to exhibit and publish works for promotional purposes. Incomplete or improperly formatted entries will be rejected.

05

Email Submission

Send Round 1 entries via email before the deadline. Subject line format: [Event Name] – School Name – Team Name. Include all members' names, grades, and contact details in the body.

06

Submission Rules

Attach files in PDF, PPT/PPTX, or JPEG/PNG format. Teams may submit only one entry (only the latest version is evaluated). Late entries require prior committee approval; shortlisted teams will be emailed.
